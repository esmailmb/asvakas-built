Anchor Bolt Design and Anchorage Systems

Anchor bolts are designed to transfer loads from structural elements into concrete substrates, and their performance is governed by both steel strength and concrete behavior.

Load Types

  • Tension (uplift)
  • Shear (lateral forces)
  • Combined loading

Interaction equations are used to evaluate combined demand.

Design Considerations

  • Effective embedment depth
  • Edge distance and spacing
  • Concrete compressive strength
  • Anchor type (cast-in vs post-installed)

Governing Failure Modes

  • Steel failure
  • Concrete breakout
  • Pull-out resistance
  • Pryout strength

Design codes prioritize ductile failure modes where possible.

Installation Effects

  • Hole cleaning
  • Adhesive curing
  • Torque application

Improper installation can invalidate design assumptions.
