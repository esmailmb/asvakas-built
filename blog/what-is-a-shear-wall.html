What is a Shear Wall?

A shear wall is a structural element designed to resist lateral forces such as wind and earthquakes. These walls are essential for maintaining the stability of buildings.

Purpose of Shear Walls

  • Excessive building sway
  • Structural instability
  • Damage during extreme loading conditions

They transfer lateral forces from floors and roofs down to the foundation.

Types of Shear Walls

  • Reinforced concrete shear walls
  • Wood shear walls
  • Steel plate shear walls

Design Considerations

  • Wind and seismic loads
  • Building height and layout
  • Load distribution
  • Connection detailing

Importance in Structural Design

  • Structural failure
  • Excessive movement
  • Damage under wind or seismic events
