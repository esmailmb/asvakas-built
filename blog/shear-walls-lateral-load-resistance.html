Shear Walls and Lateral Load Resistance

Shear walls are vertical structural elements designed to resist lateral loads and control building drift under wind and seismic actions.

Structural Behavior

Shear walls act as vertical cantilevers, transferring lateral forces from diaphragms to the foundation. They resist:

  • In-plane shear
  • Overturning moments
  • Uplift forces

System Integration

Shear walls must be integrated with:

  • Floor diaphragms
  • Hold-down systems
  • Boundary elements

Continuity between these elements is essential.

Design Considerations

  • Wall stiffness
  • Load distribution
  • Aspect ratio
  • Openings and discontinuities

Failure Mechanisms

  • Sliding failure
  • Overturning instability
  • Connection failure
  • Shear cracking
