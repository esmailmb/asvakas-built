Understanding Structural Connectors in Construction

Structural connectors are essential components used to join different structural elements and ensure that loads are safely transferred throughout a building. These connectors include anchors, clips, straps, and fasteners that work together to create a continuous load path.

In any structure, loads such as gravity, wind, and seismic forces must move through a connected system. Structural connectors ensure that these forces travel safely from one component to another and ultimately into the foundation.

What Are Structural Connectors?

Structural connectors are engineered metal components used to connect beams, columns, walls, and foundations. They are designed to resist different types of forces and maintain the integrity of the structure.

These connectors must be selected and designed based on load requirements, material compatibility, and applicable building codes.

Why They Matter

Failure in structures often occurs at connection points rather than in primary members. Even strong beams or columns can fail if the connections between them are not properly designed.

  • Continuous load transfer
  • Structural stability
  • Resistance to wind and seismic forces
  • Long-term durability

Applications

  • Wood framing systems
  • Steel structures
  • Concrete connections
  • Roof and floor systems
