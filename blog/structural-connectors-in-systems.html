Structural Connectors in Structural Engineering Systems

Structural connectors are engineered components that facilitate load transfer between structural elements and ensure continuity of the load path. While primary members such as beams and columns resist global forces, connectors govern how these forces are transmitted locally between elements.

Role in Load Transfer

  • Shear forces between members
  • Axial forces (tension/compression)
  • Bending-induced forces through eccentric connections
  • Uplift forces due to wind or seismic actions

The design of connectors must consider combined loading conditions and load path continuity to prevent localized overstress.

Limit States

  • Shear yielding
  • Bearing failure in connected materials
  • Fastener withdrawal or pull-out
  • Block shear failure
  • Connector deformation

Proper evaluation ensures that failure does not occur in a brittle or uncontrolled manner.

Engineering Importance

Connection failure is often the governing factor in structural performance. Even when members are adequately designed, inadequate connection detailing can lead to premature failure.
