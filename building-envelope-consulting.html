Building Envelope Consulting

Asvakas provides building envelope consulting for moisture control, transition detailing, enclosure assessment, air and water barrier continuity, and below-grade interface review. The focus is on technical performance, long-term durability, and coordination between systems.

What we do

Envelope consulting addresses how the building enclosure manages water, air, vapor, thermal movement, and material transitions over time. It is not limited to one membrane or one facade product. It involves understanding how multiple layers and assemblies work together and where they tend to fail when details are incomplete or incompatible.

Asvakas helps teams evaluate envelope risk at common trouble points such as parapets, penetrations, window interfaces, slab edges, below-grade transitions, roof-to-wall junctions, and repair areas where existing materials must connect to new work.

Applications and project types

This service is useful for leak investigations, enclosure rehabilitation, due diligence review, below-grade coordination, facade repair programs, roofing transitions, and restoration projects where moisture entrapment is a concern. It is also helpful when design teams need clear technical review of interface details before construction moves forward.

Why it matters

Envelope failures often appear as stains, dampness, corrosion, cracking, or interior complaints, but the underlying problem is usually a system issue rather than an isolated defect. Technical envelope review helps identify root causes, reduce repeated repair cycles, and connect moisture-control decisions back to structure and durability.

Deliverables

Technical review memos, leakage observations, transition comments, and repair-priority recommendations.

Coordination support

Detail review, interface clarification, field issue support, and project-specific envelope consulting notes.

FAQ

Is envelope consulting only for leaks?

No. It also supports durability planning, detail review, below-grade coordination, restoration work, and enclosure performance assessment before failures become obvious.

How does this differ from facade engineering?

Facade engineering focuses more directly on exterior wall support, movement, and attachment. Envelope consulting is broader and often includes moisture control, transitions, and enclosure continuity.
