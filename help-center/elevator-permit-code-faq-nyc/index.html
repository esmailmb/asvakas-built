Elevator Permit and Code FAQ

Common questions about permits, public resources, structural review, and how to prepare an NYC elevator project for consultant coordination.

Common questions

Do elevator projects in NYC require permits?

Many do. The exact filing path depends on scope, but elevator work often moves through DOB permit and DOB NOW resource paths when installation, alteration, or building-side modification is involved.

Can an existing shaft always be reused during replacement?

No. Existing geometry, pit depth, overhead clearances, support reactions, and undocumented field conditions can all affect whether reuse is feasible without added structural work.

When does modernization need structural review?

Structural review becomes more important when equipment changes support reactions, the shaft geometry changes, the pit is deepened, or the receiving structure needs confirmation.

What should owners gather before a consultation?

Existing drawings, photos, the intended scope, any filing status information, and notes on whether the building is occupied or has a usable existing shaft.
