Landmark Door, Window, and Opening FAQ

Common questions about preservation-sensitive structural review for opening changes in historic and landmarked NYC buildings.

Common questions

Does landmark opening work still need structural review?

Yes. Historic sensitivity changes how the work is approached, but lintels, hidden support, retained facade materials, and existing-building behavior still need structural review.

Is the goal to avoid changing visible historic fabric where possible?

Usually yes. The better approach is often preservation-conscious structural coordination that limits removal of significant material while still supporting the altered opening safely.

Can LPC-sensitive work still overlap DOB-facing structural issues?

Absolutely. Historic opening work often requires both preservation coordination and practical building-side engineering documentation.
